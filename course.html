Our Courses / Degrees

Associate Degree

An undergraduate degree awarded after a course of study lasting two to three years. These programs are laser focused on helping students acquire the knowledge and skills necessary to qualify for entry-level positions following graduation.

Courses

AS - Associate of Science
AT - Associate of Technology
ABA - Associate of Business Administration
AET - Associate in Engineering Technology
AAB - Associate of Applied Business
ASN - Associate of Science in Nursing
AAA - Associate of Applied Arts

Bachelor's Degree

A traditional bachelor's degree has three components: general education, core courses and elective courses. General education curriculum is usually the same for all students attending a college or university. Core courses are major specific and designed to help students become proficient is a specific field of study or discipline.

Courses

Bachelor of Engineering Technology (BSET)
Bachelor of Applied Arts (BAA)
Bachelor of Commerce (BCom, or BComm)
Bachelor of Computer Science (BCompSc)
Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSc IT)
Bachelor of Science in Law (BSL)
Bachelor of Liberal Studies

Master's Degree

A master's degree is a graduate degree students often pursue following the completion of a bachelor's degree. A master's degree focuses on a specific discipline or area of study and does not have a general education requirement. Most master's degrees are professional in nature, designed to help students develop advanced skills that will help them in their career.

Courses

Master of Accountancy (MAcc, MAc, MAcy)
Master of Economics (MEcon)
Master of Architecture (MArch)
Master of Applied Science (MASc, MAppS)
Master of Arts (MA, MA, AM, or AM)
Master of Science in Engineering (MSE)
Master of Finance (MFin)

Our Facilities

Centre For Learning Resources

The University's Centre for Learning Resources (CLR) which is the University Library, is strategically located amidst the College buildings and the Student's Halls of Residence. It can accommodate about 2,500 readers and 500 staff and postgraduate students.

Recreational Centre

A multi-purpose sport and exercise facility. We offer a range of provisions and programmes for sport, competitions and tournaments aimed at all fitness levels and capabilities. Facilities onsite include several full sized grass pitches, an artificial grass pitch (astro-turf) and a cricket wicket.

Cafeteria

There are three (3) major strategically located cafeterias on the Ace Academy Campus providing both local and continental dishes for staff and students of the University.
